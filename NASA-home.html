MARS VISITING PROGRAM



About the Red Planet

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System. It is at an average distance of 142 million miles from the Sun. A year on Mars has 687 days and each day has 24 hours and 37 minutes. The average temperature on mars is -81 degrees fahrenheit.

Mars' atmosphere is composed primarily of carbon dioxide (about 96 percent), with minor amounts of other gases such as argon and nitrogen. The atmosphere is very thin, however, and the atmospheric pressure at the surface of Mars is only about 0.6 percent of Earth’s.



About the Program

The efforts of scientist across the world have successfully helped humanity establish a multi-planetary presence. In other words, humans have established both scientific and residential bases on Mars.
We, here at NASA, are proud to declare the planet open for the public of Earth. We are looking for 50 volunteers who would be aboard the next spaceflight to Mars—the first one for tourism.
